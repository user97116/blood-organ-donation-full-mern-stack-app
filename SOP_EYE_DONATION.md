# Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) for Eye (Cornea) Donation

## ORGAN: EYE - CORNEA (Deceased Donation Only)

---

## 1. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### Donor Requirements
- **Age:** 1-75 years (all ages acceptable)
- **Time After Death:** Within 6-8 hours (ideal), up to 12 hours acceptable
- **Cause of Death:** Any cause except specific exclusions
- **Eye Condition:** No active eye infection or disease
- **Corneal Clarity:** Clear corneas preferred (assessed post-retrieval)

### Medical Exclusions
- ❌ HIV/AIDS
- ❌ Hepatitis B or C (active)
- ❌ Rabies
- ❌ Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease (CJD)
- ❌ Active septicemia
- ❌ Leukemia or lymphoma
- ❌ Intrinsic eye disease:
  - Retinoblastoma
  - Anterior segment malignancy
  - Active ocular infection
  - Corneal disease (keratoconus, dystrophies)

### Acceptable Conditions
- ✅ Diabetes
- ✅ Hypertension
- ✅ Heart disease
- ✅ Cancer (most types, except eye/blood cancers)
- ✅ Refractive surgery (LASIK, PRK) - documented
- ✅ Cataract surgery
- ✅ Wearing glasses or contact lenses
- ✅ Age-related conditions

### Special Considerations
- **Brain Death NOT Required** - Cardiac death sufficient
- **Whole Body Donation Compatible** - Eyes can be donated even if body donated to science
- **Open Casket Funeral Possible** - Prosthetic eyes placed, no visible difference

## 2. CONSENT PROCESS

### Family Approach

#### Timing
- **After Death Declaration:** Approach family sensitively
- **Trained Coordinator:** Eye bank coordinator or trained staff
- **Compassionate Communication:** Acknowledge loss, explain donation
- **No Pressure:** Respect family decision

#### Information Provided
- Eye donation process explained
- No cost to family
- Funeral arrangements not affected
- Prosthetic eyes placed (cosmetic)
- Recipient anonymity maintained
- Can help 2 people (both corneas)

### Consent Documentation
- **Written Consent Form** signed by next of kin
- **Witness Signature**
- **Medical History Questionnaire** completed
- **Donor Registry Check** (if applicable)

### Next of Kin Priority
1. Spouse
2. Adult children
3. Parents
4. Siblings
5. Legal guardian

## 4. LABORATORY TESTING

### Mandatory Serology Tests
| Test | Purpose | Timing |
|------|---------|--------|
| HIV (ELISA) | HIV screening | Pre-retrieval blood sample |
| Hepatitis B (HBsAg) | Hepatitis B screening | Pre-retrieval blood sample |
| Hepatitis C (Anti-HCV) | Hepatitis C screening | Pre-retrieval blood sample |
| Syphilis (RPR/VDRL) | Syphilis screening | Pre-retrieval blood sample |

### Blood Sample Collection
- **Timing:** Before or immediately after retrieval
- **Volume:** 10-15 mL
- **Tubes:** Red top (serum) or purple top (EDTA)
- **Labeling:** Donor identification, date, time
- **Storage:** Refrigerated until testing

### Additional Tests (If Indicated)
- Hepatitis B core antibody (Anti-HBc)
- HIV RNA (if high-risk donor)
- HCV RNA (if high-risk donor)

## 5. EYE RETRIEVAL PROCEDURE

### Preparation

#### Equipment Needed
- Sterile eye retrieval kit
- Sterile gloves and drapes
- Eyelid speculum
- Curved scissors
- Forceps
- Scalpel
- Gauze pads
- Preservation medium (Optisol-GS or similar)
- Sterile containers
- Prosthetic eyes (conformers)
- Suture material

#### Setting
- **Location:** Hospital morgue, funeral home, or donor's home
- **Lighting:** Adequate illumination
- **Privacy:** Respectful environment
- **Time Limit:** Within 6-8 hours of death (ideal)

### Retrieval Technique (Enucleation Method)

#### Step 1: Preparation (5 minutes)
- Position body supine
- Clean face and eyelids
- Drape sterile field
- Wear sterile gloves

#### Step 2: Eye Removal (10-15 minutes per eye)

**Enucleation (Whole Globe Removal):**
1. Insert eyelid speculum
2. Make 360° conjunctival incision
3. Identify and cut extraocular muscles (6 muscles)
4. Identify and cut optic nerve
5. Remove entire eyeball
6. Place in sterile container with preservation medium
7. Repeat for second eye

**Alternative: In Situ Excision (Cornea-Scleral Rim):**
1. Remove cornea with 3-4mm scleral rim
2. Faster technique
3. Used in some eye banks

#### Step 3: Prosthetic Placement (5 minutes per eye)
- Insert prosthetic eye (conformer) into socket
- Close eyelids
- Suture eyelids if needed (optional)
- Clean face
- Restore normal appearance

#### Step 4: Packaging (5 minutes)
- Place eyes in preservation medium (4°C)
- Label containers with donor information
- Seal in transport container
- Maintain cold chain

### Total Procedure Time
- **Both Eyes:** 30-40 minutes
- **Minimal Disruption:** Body ready for funeral home

## 6. TISSUE PRESERVATION

### Preservation Medium
- **Optisol-GS:** Most common (14-day preservation)
- **Life 4°C:** Alternative medium
- **Dexsol:** Older medium (7-day preservation)

### Storage Conditions
- **Temperature:** 2-8°C (refrigerated, not frozen)
- **Container:** Sterile vial with preservation medium
- **Labeling:** Donor ID, retrieval date/time, eye bank info

### Preservation Duration
- **Optisol-GS:** Up to 14 days
- **Ideal Use:** Within 7 days
- **Quality Decreases:** After 10 days

---

## 7. EYE BANK PROCESSING

### Tissue Arrival at Eye Bank

#### Inspection
- Verify donor identification
- Check preservation medium
- Assess tissue condition
- Document arrival time

#### Slit Lamp Examination
- **Corneal Clarity:** Graded 0-4 (0=clear, 4=opaque)
- **Endothelial Cell Count:** >2000 cells/mm² (ideal >2500)
- **Corneal Thickness:** 500-600 microns
- **Epithelial Defects:** Noted
- **Stromal Abnormalities:** Scars, infiltrates
- **Descemet's Membrane:** Integrity assessed

#### Endothelial Cell Analysis
- **Specular Microscopy:** Cell count and morphology
- **Cell Density:** Minimum 2000 cells/mm²
- **Cell Morphology:** Hexagonal shape preferred
- **Coefficient of Variation:** <40% (uniformity)

### Tissue Grading
- **Excellent:** ECC >2500, clear cornea, no defects
- **Good:** ECC 2000-2500, minimal defects
- **Fair:** ECC 1800-2000, some defects (may be used)
- **Poor:** ECC <1800, significant defects (not suitable)

## 8. RECIPIENT MATCHING

### Indications for Corneal Transplant

#### Common Conditions
- **Keratoconus:** Cone-shaped cornea
- **Fuchs' Dystrophy:** Endothelial dysfunction
- **Corneal Scarring:** Trauma, infection, surgery
- **Corneal Edema:** Swelling from various causes
- **Corneal Ulcers:** Non-healing ulcers
- **Corneal Dystrophies:** Genetic conditions
- **Failed Previous Transplant:** Rejection or failure

### Matching Criteria
- **Blood Type:** NOT required (cornea is immune-privileged)
- **Tissue Typing:** NOT required
- **Size Match:** Donor cornea sized to recipient need
- **Waiting List:** First come, first served (usually)
- **Urgency:** Emergency cases prioritized

### Allocation Priority
1. **Emergency Cases:** Corneal perforation, severe infection
2. **Pediatric Cases:** Children prioritized
3. **Waiting Time:** Longer wait = higher priority
4. **Geographic Proximity:** Local > regional > national

## 9. CORNEAL TRANSPLANT PROCEDURE

### Types of Corneal Transplant

#### Penetrating Keratoplasty (PK) - Full Thickness
- **Indication:** Full-thickness corneal disease
- **Procedure:** Replace entire cornea (all layers)
- **Recovery:** 6-12 months
- **Success Rate:** 80-90% at 5 years

#### Endothelial Keratoplasty (EK) - Partial Thickness
- **DSEK (Descemet's Stripping Endothelial Keratoplasty):**
  - Replace endothelium and posterior stroma
  - Faster recovery (3-6 months)
  - Better visual outcomes
  
- **DMEK (Descemet's Membrane Endothelial Keratoplasty):**
  - Replace only endothelium and Descemet's membrane
  - Fastest recovery (1-3 months)
  - Best visual outcomes
  - Technically challenging

#### Anterior Lamellar Keratoplasty (ALK) - Partial Thickness
- **DALK (Deep Anterior Lamellar Keratoplasty):**
  - Replace anterior layers, preserve endothelium
  - Lower rejection risk
  - Used for keratoconus, scars

### Surgical Procedure (PK Example)

#### Pre-Operative (30 minutes)
- Anesthesia (local or general)
- Eye preparation and draping
- Donor cornea prepared (sized)

#### Surgery (60-90 minutes)
1. **Trephination:** Remove recipient cornea (circular cut)
2. **Donor Preparation:** Size donor cornea to match
3. **Suturing:** Place donor cornea, suture with 16-24 interrupted or running sutures
4. **Anterior Chamber:** Reform with balanced salt solution
5. **Check:** Ensure watertight closure

#### Post-Operative
- Eye patch and shield
- Topical antibiotics and steroids
- Discharge same day or next day

## 10. POST-TRANSPLANT CARE (Recipient)

### Immediate Post-Op (0-7 days)
- **Medications:**
  - Antibiotic drops (4x/day)
  - Steroid drops (4-6x/day)
  - Cycloplegic drops (2x/day)
  
- **Activity:**
  - No rubbing eyes
  - Wear eye shield at night
  - Avoid strenuous activity
  
- **Follow-Up:**
  - Day 1 post-op visit
  - Week 1 visit

### Short-Term (1-3 months)
- **Medications:**
  - Taper steroids gradually
  - Continue antibiotics (2-4 weeks)
  
- **Monitoring:**
  - Weekly visits initially
  - Watch for rejection signs
  - Suture management

### Long-Term (3-12 months)
- **Suture Removal:**
  - Interrupted sutures: Remove at 3-6 months
  - Running suture: Remove at 12-18 months
  
- **Medications:**
  - Low-dose steroid drops (long-term)
  
- **Follow-Up:**
  - Monthly visits
  - Vision improvement gradual

### Lifelong
- **Annual Eye Exams**
- **Rejection Surveillance**
- **Steroid Drops:** May need indefinitely (low dose)

## 11. COMPLICATIONS

### Donor-Related (Rare)
- Tissue contamination (prevented by sterile technique)
- Disease transmission (prevented by screening)
- Poor tissue quality (detected at eye bank)

### Recipient Complications

#### Early (0-3 months)
| Complication | Incidence | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| Infection | 1-2% | Antibiotics, possible graft removal |
| Wound Leak | 2-5% | Resuturing |
| High Intraocular Pressure | 10-15% | Glaucoma medications |
| Epithelial Defect | 5-10% | Lubrication, bandage contact lens |

#### Late (>3 months)
| Complication | Incidence | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| Rejection | 10-30% | Increase steroids, may need re-transplant |
| Astigmatism | Common | Glasses, contact lenses, suture adjustment |
| Graft Failure | 10-20% at 5 years | Re-transplant |
| Glaucoma | 10-15% | Medications, surgery |
| Cataract | 20-30% | Cataract surgery |

### Rejection Signs (RSVP Mnemonic)
- **R**edness
- **S**ensitivity to light
- **V**ision decrease
- **P**ain

**Action:** Seek immediate medical attention if any rejection signs

## 12. OUTCOMES

### Success Rates
- **First Transplant:** 80-90% clear graft at 5 years
- **Repeat Transplant:** 50-70% success
- **Low-Risk Patients:** >90% success (keratoconus, Fuchs')
- **High-Risk Patients:** 50-60% success (vascularized corneas, previous rejections)

### Visual Outcomes
- **20/40 or Better:** 60-70% of patients
- **20/200 or Better:** 90% of patients
- **Improvement:** Most patients see better than pre-transplant

### Factors Affecting Success
- **Positive Factors:**
  - Young recipient age
  - First transplant
  - Non-vascularized cornea
  - Good compliance with medications
  
- **Negative Factors:**
  - Previous rejection
  - Vascularized cornea
  - Glaucoma
  - Dry eye
  - Poor compliance

## 13. ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Consent
- **Presumed Consent:** Some countries (opt-out system)
- **Informed Consent:** Most countries (opt-in system)
- **Family Consent:** Required in most cases
- **Donor Registry:** Respect donor's wishes

### Allocation
- **Fair Distribution:** Equitable access
- **Medical Urgency:** Emergency cases prioritized
- **No Discrimination:** Race, religion, socioeconomic status

### Commercialization
- ❌ **No Payment:** Organ/tissue sales prohibited
- ✅ **Reasonable Costs:** Eye bank processing fees acceptable
- ✅ **Transplant Costs:** Covered by recipient's insurance

## 14. QUALITY ASSURANCE

### Eye Bank Standards
- **Accreditation:** Eye Bank Association of America (EBAA) or equivalent
- **Sterile Technique:** Prevent contamination
- **Serology Testing:** 100% of donors
- **Tissue Evaluation:** Slit lamp and endothelial cell count
- **Record Keeping:** Complete documentation
- **Adverse Event Reporting:** Track complications

### Outcome Monitoring
- Transplant success rates tracked
- Rejection rates monitored
- Infection rates reviewed
- Quality improvement initiatives

---

## 15. SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Pediatric Donors
- **All Ages Acceptable:** Infants to elderly
- **Smaller Eyes:** Infant corneas smaller (may be used for children)
- **Consent:** Parents/guardians provide consent

### High-Risk Donors
- **Increased Testing:** Additional serology
- **Informed Consent:** Recipient informed of risk
- **Used When:** Benefit outweighs risk (emergency cases)

### Refractive Surgery Donors
- **LASIK/PRK:** Acceptable if documented
- **Corneal Thickness:** May be thinner
- **Recipient Informed:** Disclose refractive surgery history

## ADDITIONAL INFORMATION

### One Donor Can Help Multiple People
- **2 Corneal Transplants:** Both corneas used
- **Sclera:** Used for glaucoma or retinal surgery
- **Research:** Tissue not suitable for transplant may be used for research (with consent)

### Donation Does Not Affect Funeral
- **Open Casket:** Possible with prosthetic eyes
- **No Visible Difference:** Eyelids closed naturally
- **No Delay:** Body released to funeral home promptly

### Gift of Sight
- Corneal transplant restores vision
- Improves quality of life
- Allows return to work, driving, reading
- One of the most successful transplant procedures
