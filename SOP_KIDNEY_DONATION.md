# Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) for Kidney Donation

## ORGAN: KIDNEY (Living & Deceased Donation)

---

## 1. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### Living Donor Requirements
- **Age:** 18-65 years (ideal 25-55 years)
- **BMI:** <35 kg/m²
- **Blood Pressure:** <140/90 mmHg (controlled if on medication)
- **No Diabetes:** Or well-controlled diabetes (HbA1c <6.5%)
- **Two Healthy Kidneys:** Both kidneys functioning normally
- **GFR:** >80 mL/min/1.73m²
- **No Proteinuria:** Urine protein <150 mg/24hr
- **Voluntary Consent:** No coercion or financial incentive
- **Psychological Stability:** Cleared by psychiatrist

### Deceased Donor Requirements
- **Brain Death Certification:** By two independent physicians
- **Age:** Typically <70 years (extended criteria up to 75)
- **Kidney Function:** Adequate renal function
- **No Active Infection:** HIV, Hepatitis B/C negative
- **No Kidney Disease:** No chronic kidney disease

### Medical Exclusions (Living Donors)
- ❌ Chronic kidney disease (any stage)
- ❌ Uncontrolled hypertension
- ❌ Diabetes (Type 1 or uncontrolled Type 2)
- ❌ Active cancer (within 5 years)
- ❌ HIV, Hepatitis B/C positive
- ❌ Kidney stones (recurrent)
- ❌ Polycystic kidney disease
- ❌ Glomerulonephritis
- ❌ Pregnancy (defer until 6 months postpartum)
- ❌ Severe obesity (BMI >35)
- ❌ Active substance abuse

## 2. PRE-DONATION EVALUATION (Living Donors)

### Phase 1: Initial Screening (Week 1-2)

#### Medical History
- Complete health questionnaire
- Family medical history
- Medication review
- Surgical history
- Lifestyle assessment (smoking, alcohol, drugs)

#### Physical Examination
- Height, weight, BMI
- Blood pressure (multiple readings)
- Cardiovascular examination
- Abdominal examination
- General health assessment

#### Basic Laboratory Tests
| Test | Normal Range | Purpose |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Complete Blood Count | Normal | Anemia, infection screening |
| Blood Type | ABO, Rh | Compatibility matching |
| Serum Creatinine | 0.6-1.2 mg/dL | Kidney function |
| eGFR | >80 mL/min | Kidney filtration rate |
| Fasting Glucose | 70-100 mg/dL | Diabetes screening |
| HbA1c | <5.7% | Long-term glucose control |
| Lipid Profile | Normal | Cardiovascular risk |
| Liver Function Tests | Normal | General health |
| Urinalysis | No protein/blood | Kidney health |
| 24-Hour Urine | Protein <150mg | Kidney function |

#### Infectious Disease Screening
- HIV (ELISA and Western Blot)
- Hepatitis B (HBsAg, Anti-HBc, Anti-HBs)
- Hepatitis C (Anti-HCV, HCV RNA)
- Syphilis (RPR/VDRL)
- CMV (IgG, IgM)
- EBV (IgG, IgM)
- Tuberculosis (PPD or QuantiFERON)

### Phase 2: Detailed Assessment (Week 3-4)

#### Kidney Imaging
- **CT Angiography (CTA):**
  - Kidney size and anatomy
  - Vascular anatomy (arteries, veins)
  - Presence of cysts or masses
  - Ureter anatomy
  - Selection of kidney to donate (usually left)

- **Nuclear Medicine Scan (DTPA/MAG3):**
  - Split kidney function (each kidney >40%)
  - Differential function assessment

#### Cardiovascular Assessment
- **ECG:** Baseline cardiac rhythm
- **Echocardiogram:** If age >50 or risk factors
- **Stress Test:** If age >50 or cardiac risk factors
- **Chest X-Ray:** Baseline imaging

#### Additional Tests
- **24-Hour Ambulatory Blood Pressure:** Confirm BP control
- **Glucose Tolerance Test:** If borderline diabetes
- **Urine Culture:** Rule out infection
- **Pregnancy Test:** For women of childbearing age

### Phase 3: Psychological & Social Evaluation (Week 4-5)

#### Psychological Assessment
- **Mental Health Screening:**
  - Depression screening (PHQ-9)
  - Anxiety assessment (GAD-7)
  - Substance abuse history
  - Coping mechanisms
  
- **Motivation Assessment:**
  - Reasons for donation
  - Understanding of risks
  - Expectations post-donation
  - Pressure or coercion screening

- **Cognitive Assessment:**
  - Understanding of procedure
  - Informed consent capacity
  - Decision-making ability

#### Social Work Evaluation
- **Financial Assessment:**
  - Employment status
  - Insurance coverage
  - Financial stability
  - Lost wages compensation

- **Support System:**
  - Family support
  - Caregiver availability
  - Transportation access
  - Post-operative care plan

- **Living Situation:**
  - Housing stability
  - Distance from hospital
  - Recovery environment

## 3. RECIPIENT MATCHING

### Compatibility Testing

#### Blood Type Compatibility
| Donor | Can Donate To |
|-------|---------------|
| O | O, A, B, AB (Universal donor) |
| A | A, AB |
| B | B, AB |
| AB | AB only |

#### Tissue Typing (HLA)
- **HLA-A, HLA-B, HLA-DR** matching
- 6-antigen match (best outcome)
- 0-antigen mismatch acceptable with immunosuppression

#### Cross-Match Testing
- **Lymphocyte Cross-Match:** Must be negative
- **Flow Cytometry Cross-Match:** Detects low-level antibodies
- **Virtual Cross-Match:** Computer-based prediction

#### Antibody Screening
- **Panel Reactive Antibody (PRA):** Measures sensitization
- **Donor-Specific Antibodies (DSA):** Must be absent or low

## 4. INFORMED CONSENT PROCESS

### Information Provided to Donor

#### Surgical Risks (Short-Term)
- Bleeding (1-2%)
- Infection (1-2%)
- Blood clots (0.5%)
- Pneumonia (1%)
- Wound complications (2-3%)
- Conversion to open surgery (1-2%)
- Death (0.03% - 3 in 10,000)

#### Long-Term Risks
- **Kidney Function:** Slight decrease in GFR (acceptable)
- **Hypertension:** 5-10% increased risk
- **Proteinuria:** Small increase in protein excretion
- **End-Stage Renal Disease:** 0.5% lifetime risk (vs 0.3% general population)
- **Pregnancy:** Slightly increased risk of preeclampsia

#### Benefits
- Saving a life
- Improved quality of life for recipient
- Psychological satisfaction
- No long-term dialysis for recipient

#### Alternatives
- Deceased donor transplant (longer wait)
- Dialysis continuation
- Other living donors

### Consent Documentation
- Written informed consent
- Witness signatures
- Independent donor advocate review
- Right to withdraw at any time

## 5. SURGICAL PROCEDURE (Living Donor)

### Pre-Operative Preparation

#### Day Before Surgery
- Hospital admission
- Final laboratory tests
- Anesthesia consultation
- NPO (nothing by mouth) after midnight
- IV hydration started

#### Morning of Surgery
- Vital signs monitoring
- IV antibiotics (prophylactic)
- Anticoagulation (DVT prophylaxis)
- Consent reconfirmation

### Laparoscopic Nephrectomy (Preferred Method)

#### Anesthesia
- General anesthesia
- Endotracheal intubation
- Arterial line for blood pressure monitoring
- Foley catheter for urine output

#### Surgical Steps (2-3 hours)

**Step 1: Port Placement (15 minutes)**
- Patient positioned in lateral decubitus (side-lying)
- 3-4 small incisions (5-12 mm)
- Camera port inserted
- Working ports placed
- CO₂ insufflation for visualization

**Step 2: Kidney Mobilization (60-90 minutes)**
- Colon reflected medially
- Kidney identified and mobilized
- Ureter dissected and preserved
- Renal artery identified
- Renal vein identified
- Adrenal gland preserved
- Surrounding tissue dissected

**Step 3: Vascular Control (15 minutes)**
- Renal artery isolated
- Renal vein isolated
- Ureter divided (distal end)
- Vascular clips/staples prepared

**Step 4: Kidney Removal (10 minutes)**
- Small extraction incision (6-8 cm) in lower abdomen
- Renal artery clamped and divided
- Renal vein clamped and divided
- Kidney removed through extraction site
- Immediate cold preservation

**Step 5: Closure (20 minutes)**
- Hemostasis confirmed
- Ports removed
- Incisions closed
- Dressing applied

### Open Nephrectomy (Alternative)
- Larger incision (15-20 cm)
- Direct visualization
- Used if anatomy complex or laparoscopy not feasible
- Longer recovery time

## 6. KIDNEY PRESERVATION

### Immediate Preservation
- **Flush with Cold Preservation Solution:**
  - University of Wisconsin (UW) solution
  - Histidine-Tryptophan-Ketoglutarate (HTK) solution
  - Temperature: 4°C

- **Packaging:**
  - Triple-bagged in sterile containers
  - Surrounded by ice slush
  - Labeled with donor information

### Cold Ischemic Time
- **Living Donor:** Typically <1 hour (immediate transplant)
- **Deceased Donor:** Up to 24-36 hours acceptable
- **Ideal:** <12 hours for best outcomes

## 7. POST-OPERATIVE CARE (Living Donor)

### Immediate Post-Op (0-24 hours)

#### Recovery Room (2-4 hours)
- Vital signs every 15 minutes
- Pain management (IV opioids)
- Nausea control
- Oxygen supplementation
- Foley catheter monitoring

#### Hospital Ward (1-3 days)
- **Pain Management:**
  - Patient-controlled analgesia (PCA)
  - Transition to oral pain medications
  - Pain score monitoring

- **Mobilization:**
  - Sit up in bed (6 hours post-op)
  - Walk with assistance (12 hours post-op)
  - Independent walking (24 hours post-op)

- **Diet:**
  - Clear liquids (6 hours post-op)
  - Regular diet (24 hours post-op)
  - Encourage fluid intake

- **Monitoring:**
  - Vital signs every 4 hours
  - Urine output monitoring
  - Wound inspection
  - Laboratory tests (creatinine, hemoglobin)

### Hospital Discharge (Day 2-3)
- Pain controlled with oral medications
- Tolerating regular diet
- Ambulating independently
- No fever or complications
- Creatinine stable or improving

### Discharge Instructions
- **Activity:**
  - No heavy lifting (>10 lbs) for 6 weeks
  - No driving for 2 weeks
  - Gradual return to activities
  - No contact sports for 3 months

- **Wound Care:**
  - Keep incisions clean and dry
  - Shower after 48 hours (no bath)
  - Watch for signs of infection

- **Medications:**
  - Pain medications as needed
  - Stool softeners
  - No NSAIDs (ibuprofen, aspirin) for 6 weeks

- **Diet:**
  - Regular diet
  - Adequate hydration (8-10 glasses water/day)
  - No dietary restrictions

## 8. FOLLOW-UP CARE (Living Donor)

### Short-Term Follow-Up

#### Week 1-2
- **Clinic Visit:**
  - Wound check
  - Pain assessment
  - Blood pressure check
  - Laboratory tests (creatinine, CBC)

#### Week 4-6
- **Clinic Visit:**
  - Wound healing assessment
  - Activity clearance
  - Blood pressure monitoring
  - Laboratory tests (creatinine, GFR)

#### Month 3
- **Clinic Visit:**
  - Full activity clearance
  - Blood pressure check
  - Laboratory tests
  - Return to work clearance

### Long-Term Follow-Up (Lifelong)

#### Year 1
- Visits at 6 months and 12 months
- Blood pressure monitoring
- Kidney function tests (creatinine, GFR)
- Urinalysis (protein screening)

#### Year 2 and Beyond
- **Annual Visits:**
  - Blood pressure check
  - Kidney function tests
  - Urinalysis
  - General health assessment

#### Monitoring Parameters
| Test | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Blood Pressure | Every visit | Hypertension screening |
| Serum Creatinine | Annually | Kidney function |
| eGFR | Annually | Filtration rate |
| Urinalysis | Annually | Protein screening |
| Urine Protein | If proteinuria | Quantification |

## 9. RECIPIENT TRANSPLANT PROCEDURE

### Pre-Transplant Preparation
- Recipient prepared in OR
- Anesthesia induced
- Lower abdominal incision (right or left iliac fossa)
- Iliac vessels exposed

### Transplant Procedure (2-3 hours)

**Step 1: Vascular Anastomosis**
- Renal artery connected to external iliac artery
- Renal vein connected to external iliac vein
- Clamps removed, kidney perfused

**Step 2: Ureter Anastomosis**
- Ureter connected to bladder
- Stent placed (temporary)

**Step 3: Closure**
- Hemostasis confirmed
- Drain placed
- Incision closed

### Immediate Post-Transplant
- Kidney function monitored (urine output)
- Immunosuppression started
- ICU or transplant ward

## 10. COMPLICATIONS AND MANAGEMENT

### Donor Complications

#### Immediate (0-30 days)
| Complication | Incidence | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| Bleeding | 1-2% | Transfusion, reoperation if severe |
| Infection | 1-2% | Antibiotics |
| Pneumonia | 1% | Antibiotics, respiratory therapy |
| Blood Clots (DVT/PE) | 0.5% | Anticoagulation |
| Wound Issues | 2-3% | Wound care, antibiotics |
| Ileus | 5% | Bowel rest, NG tube |

#### Long-Term (>30 days)
| Complication | Incidence | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| Hypertension | 5-10% | Antihypertensive medications |
| Proteinuria | 5% | ACE inhibitors, monitoring |
| Chronic Pain | 2-3% | Pain management |
| Hernia | 1-2% | Surgical repair |
| ESRD | 0.5% lifetime | Dialysis, transplant |

### Recipient Complications
- Delayed graft function (10-20%)
- Acute rejection (10-15%)
- Chronic rejection (long-term)
- Infection (immunosuppression)
- Medication side effects

## 11. DECEASED DONOR KIDNEY DONATION

### Brain Death Criteria
- Same as heart donation (see Heart SOP)
- Two independent physician certifications
- Neurological examination
- Apnea test

### Donor Management
- Hemodynamic stability
- Urine output >1 mL/kg/hr
- Minimize vasopressor use
- Maintain normothermia

### Retrieval Procedure
- Multi-organ procurement (if applicable)
- Cold perfusion with preservation solution
- En bloc removal (both kidneys)
- Vascular and ureteral preservation

### Allocation
- UNOS waiting list
- Points based on:
  - Waiting time
  - HLA matching
  - PRA level
  - Age
  - Geographic proximity

## 12. ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Living Donation Ethics

#### Principles
- **Autonomy:** Donor's free choice
- **Beneficence:** Benefit to recipient
- **Non-Maleficence:** Minimize donor harm
- **Justice:** Fair access to transplantation

#### Prohibited Practices
- ❌ Financial compensation (except expenses)
- ❌ Coercion or pressure
- ❌ Donation by minors (except rare cases)
- ❌ Incompetent donors
- ❌ Directed donation to strangers (in some regions)

#### Independent Donor Advocate
- Separate from transplant team
- Protects donor interests
- Ensures informed consent
- Can stop process if concerns

### Deceased Donation Ethics
- Donation after brain death (DBD)
- Donation after cardiac death (DCD) - in some centers
- Family consent required
- No hastening of death

## 13. FINANCIAL ASPECTS

### Costs Covered (Living Donation)
- ✅ Medical evaluation
- ✅ Surgery and hospitalization
- ✅ Post-operative care
- ✅ Complications related to donation
- ✅ Travel expenses (varies by program)
- ✅ Lost wages (varies by program)

### Costs NOT Covered
- ❌ Long-term health insurance premiums
- ❌ Life insurance premiums
- ❌ Disability insurance
- ❌ Non-donation-related medical care

### Insurance
- Recipient's insurance covers donor costs
- Donor's insurance not billed
- Pre-existing condition protection (in most countries)

## 14. QUALITY ASSURANCE

### Hospital Requirements
- **Transplant Center Accreditation**
- **Experienced Surgical Team** (>25 transplants/year)
- **Multidisciplinary Team:**
  - Transplant surgeons
  - Nephrologists
  - Transplant coordinators
  - Social workers
  - Psychiatrists
  - Financial counselors

### Outcome Monitoring
- Donor outcomes tracked
- Recipient outcomes tracked
- Complication rates reviewed
- Quality improvement initiatives

## 15. DONOR RIGHTS

### Legal Rights
- Right to withdraw consent anytime before surgery
- Right to confidentiality
- Right to independent medical evaluation
- Right to donor advocate
- Protection from discrimination

### Medical Rights
- Lifelong follow-up care
- Priority on transplant list (if needed in future)
- Treatment of donation-related complications
