# Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) for Heart Donation

## ORGAN: HEART (Deceased Donation Only)

---

## 1. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### Donor Requirements
- **Age:** Typically 18-55 years (may extend to 65 in exceptional cases)
- **Brain Death Certification:** Required by medical board
- **Heart Function:** Normal cardiac function confirmed by echocardiogram
- **No Active Infections:** HIV, Hepatitis B/C negative
- **No Heart Disease History:** No coronary artery disease, cardiomyopathy, or significant arrhythmias
- **Hemodynamic Stability:** Maintained with minimal vasopressor support

### Medical Exclusions
- ❌ Active cardiac disease
- ❌ Severe coronary artery disease
- ❌ Significant cardiac trauma
- ❌ Prolonged cardiac arrest (>20 minutes)
- ❌ Severe left ventricular dysfunction (EF <45%)
- ❌ Active malignancy (except certain brain tumors)
- ❌ Untreated sepsis

---

## 2. BRAIN DEATH CERTIFICATION

### Legal Requirements
- **Two Independent Physicians** must certify brain death
- **Neurological Examination** confirming absence of:
  - Brainstem reflexes
  - Spontaneous respiration
  - Response to painful stimuli
  - Pupillary light reflex
  
- **Apnea Test:** Confirms no respiratory drive
- **Confirmatory Tests** (if needed):
  - EEG (Electroencephalogram)
  - Cerebral angiography
  - Transcranial Doppler

### Documentation
- Brain death certificate signed by two doctors
- Time of death declaration
- Family consent for organ donation
- Legal authorization documents

---

## 3. PRE-DONATION EVALUATION

### Cardiac Assessment

#### Echocardiogram (ECHO)
- **Ejection Fraction (EF):** Must be >50%
- **Wall Motion:** Normal regional wall motion
- **Valve Function:** No significant valvular disease
- **Chamber Size:** Normal cardiac chambers
- **No Thrombus:** Absence of intracardiac clots

#### Electrocardiogram (ECG)
- Rhythm assessment
- No significant ST-T changes
- QRS duration normal
- No evidence of acute ischemia

#### Cardiac Enzymes
- Troponin levels (mild elevation acceptable)
- CK-MB levels
- BNP/NT-proBNP

### Laboratory Tests
| Test | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Blood Type | ABO and Rh typing |
| Tissue Typing | HLA matching |
| Cross-Match | Compatibility with recipient |
| HIV | Negative |
| Hepatitis B | Negative |
| Hepatitis C | Negative |
| CMV | Status documented |
| Toxoplasma | Status documented |

### Imaging Studies
- **Chest X-Ray:** Normal cardiac silhouette
- **Coronary Angiography:** May be required for donors >40 years
- **CT Chest:** If trauma suspected

---

## 4. DONOR MANAGEMENT

### Hemodynamic Goals
- **Blood Pressure:** Systolic >100 mmHg
- **Heart Rate:** 60-120 bpm
- **CVP:** 6-10 mmHg
- **Urine Output:** >1 ml/kg/hr
- **Temperature:** Maintain >36°C

### Medications
- **Vasopressors:** Minimize use (dopamine <10 mcg/kg/min preferred)
- **Hormonal Therapy:**
  - Thyroid hormone (T3/T4)
  - Vasopressin
  - Methylprednisolone
  - Insulin (maintain glucose 120-180 mg/dL)

### Monitoring
- Continuous ECG monitoring
- Arterial line for blood pressure
- Central venous pressure monitoring
- Serial echocardiograms
- Hourly urine output

---

## 5. ORGAN RETRIEVAL PROCEDURE

### Surgical Team
- **Cardiothoracic Surgeon** (lead)
- **Surgical Assistants** (2-3)
- **Anesthesiologist**
- **Perfusionist**
- **Transplant Coordinator**
- **Operating Room Nurses**

### Retrieval Process

#### Step 1: Preparation (30 minutes)
- Patient positioned supine
- Median sternotomy incision
- Pericardium opened
- Heart inspected for abnormalities

#### Step 2: Cannulation (15 minutes)
- Aortic cannulation
- Pulmonary artery cannulation
- Cardioplegia solution prepared (cold crystalloid)

#### Step 3: Cross-Clamping (5 minutes)
- Aorta cross-clamped
- Cardioplegia infused (cold potassium solution)
- Heart arrests in diastole
- Topical ice slush applied

#### Step 4: Excision (20 minutes)
- Superior vena cava divided
- Inferior vena cava divided
- Pulmonary veins divided
- Aorta divided
- Pulmonary artery divided
- Heart removed

#### Step 5: Preservation (Immediate)
- Heart placed in cold preservation solution (4°C)
- Triple-bagged in sterile containers
- Placed in ice-filled cooler
- Transported immediately

### Ischemic Time Limits
- **Cold Ischemic Time:** Maximum 4-6 hours
- **Ideal Time:** <4 hours from retrieval to transplant
- **Critical:** Every minute counts

---

## 6. TRANSPORTATION

### Packaging
1. **First Bag:** Sterile bag with preservation solution
2. **Second Bag:** Sterile outer bag
3. **Third Bag:** Labeled container with donor information
4. **Ice Container:** Maintain 4°C temperature

### Transport Team
- Transplant surgeon or coordinator
- Preservation solution monitoring
- Temperature monitoring
- Time documentation

### Documentation During Transport
- Retrieval time
- Preservation solution type
- Temperature logs
- Expected arrival time at recipient hospital

---

## 7. RECIPIENT MATCHING

### Compatibility Criteria
- **Blood Type:** ABO compatible (identical preferred)
- **Size Match:** Donor-recipient weight ratio 0.8-2.0
- **Tissue Typing:** HLA matching (not mandatory but preferred)
- **Cross-Match:** Negative lymphocyte cross-match
- **Urgency:** UNOS status (1A highest priority)

### Allocation Priority
1. Status 1A (ICU, mechanical support)
2. Status 1B (continuous IV inotropes)
3. Status 2 (stable on oral medications)
4. Geographic proximity (local > regional > national)

---

## 8. FAMILY CONSENT PROCESS

### Approach to Family
- **Timing:** After brain death declaration, allow time for grief
- **Trained Coordinator:** Organ procurement coordinator leads discussion
- **Separate Teams:** Treating physicians ≠ transplant team
- **Compassionate Communication:** Acknowledge loss, explain donation process
- **No Pressure:** Respect family decision

### Information Provided
- Brain death explanation
- Organ donation process
- No cost to donor family
- Funeral arrangements not affected
- Recipient anonymity maintained
- Family can receive update on recipients (if desired)

### Consent Documentation
- Written consent form signed
- Witness signatures
- Legal authorization
- Medical examiner clearance (if applicable)

---

## 9. POST-RETRIEVAL CARE

### Donor Body Care
- Surgical incision closed properly
- Body prepared for funeral home
- Respectful handling
- Family viewing allowed if desired

### Documentation
- Complete surgical report
- Organ quality assessment
- Preservation details
- Transport documentation
- Recipient hospital notification

---

## 10. RISKS AND COMPLICATIONS

### For Donor Family
- **Emotional Impact:** Grief, loss, decision burden
- **Delayed Funeral:** May delay by 24-48 hours
- **No Medical Risks:** Donor is deceased

### For Recipient
- **Rejection:** Acute or chronic rejection
- **Infection:** Immunosuppression increases risk
- **Primary Graft Failure:** Heart doesn't function (rare)
- **Coronary Allograft Vasculopathy:** Long-term complication
- **Malignancy:** Increased risk with immunosuppression

---

## 11. ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Principles
- **Autonomy:** Respect donor's prior wishes
- **Beneficence:** Maximize benefit to recipients
- **Non-Maleficence:** Do no harm
- **Justice:** Fair organ allocation

### Prohibited Practices
- ❌ Donation before brain death declaration
- ❌ Hastening death for organ procurement
- ❌ Directed donation (except family)
- ❌ Financial compensation
- ❌ Coercion of family

### Conflict of Interest
- Treating team ≠ Transplant team
- Independent brain death certification
- No financial incentives

---

## 12. QUALITY ASSURANCE

### Hospital Requirements
- **Level 1 Trauma Center** or equivalent
- **Cardiothoracic Surgery** capability
- **ICU Facilities** with cardiac monitoring
- **24/7 Laboratory** services
- **Organ Procurement Organization (OPO)** partnership

### Team Qualifications
- Board-certified cardiothoracic surgeons
- Experienced transplant coordinators
- Trained ICU staff
- Ethics committee oversight

### Documentation Standards
- Complete medical records
- Brain death certification
- Consent forms
- Surgical reports
- Quality metrics tracking

---

## 13. RECIPIENT TRANSPLANT PROCEDURE

### Pre-Transplant
- Recipient prepared in OR
- Anesthesia induced
- Cardiopulmonary bypass initiated
- Diseased heart removed

### Transplant (Orthotopic)
- **Biatrial Technique** or **Bicaval Technique**
- Left atrium anastomosis
- Right atrium anastomosis
- Pulmonary artery anastomosis
- Aortic anastomosis
- De-airing of cardiac chambers
- Cross-clamp removed
- Heart reperfused
- Spontaneous rhythm or defibrillation

### Post-Transplant
- Weaning from bypass
- Hemodynamic stabilization
- ICU transfer
- Immunosuppression initiated

---

## 14. FOLLOW-UP AND OUTCOMES

### Recipient Follow-Up
- **First Year:** Weekly to monthly visits
- **Long-Term:** Every 3-6 months
- **Endomyocardial Biopsy:** Regular rejection surveillance
- **Echocardiograms:** Cardiac function monitoring
- **Coronary Angiography:** Annual screening

### Success Rates
- **1-Year Survival:** ~90%
- **5-Year Survival:** ~75%
- **10-Year Survival:** ~55%

### Donor Family Support
- Grief counseling available
- Recipient outcome updates (anonymous)
- Donor memorial services
- Support groups

---

## 15. EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

### Donor Instability
- Aggressive hemodynamic support
- Echocardiogram reassessment
- Consider expedited retrieval
- Notify recipient hospital

### Transport Delays
- Maintain cold ischemic time <6 hours
- Alternative transport arrangements
- Direct communication with recipient team

### Recipient Complications
- Backup recipient identified
- Alternative allocation if primary unsuitable

2. Voluntary consent without coercion
3. Understanding of the retrieval process
4. No financial compensation involved
