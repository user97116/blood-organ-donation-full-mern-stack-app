# Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) for Organ Donation

## 1. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### General Requirements
- Age: 18-65 years
- Sound mental and physical health
- Free from infectious diseases (HIV, Hepatitis B/C, TB)
- No history of cancer (except certain skin cancers)
- Voluntary consent without coercion

### Organ-Specific Criteria

#### KIDNEY DONATION
- Two healthy functioning kidneys
- Normal kidney function (GFR > 80 mL/min)
- No diabetes or controlled diabetes
- Blood pressure < 140/90 mmHg
- BMI < 35
- No kidney stones or chronic kidney disease

#### LIVER DONATION
- Healthy liver with no cirrhosis or fatty liver disease
- No history of liver disease
- No excessive alcohol consumption
- Compatible blood type with recipient
- Liver volume adequate for donation (assessed via CT scan)

#### HEART DONATION
- Applicable only for deceased donation
- Brain death certification by medical board
- Heart function normal (assessed via echocardiogram)
- No history of heart disease
- Age typically < 55 years

## 2. MEDICAL EVALUATION PROCESS

### Step 1: Initial Screening (Day 1-3)
- Complete medical history
- Physical examination
- Blood type and tissue typing
- Basic blood tests (CBC, kidney function, liver function)
- Chest X-ray and ECG

### Step 2: Detailed Assessment (Week 1-2)
- Organ-specific imaging (CT/MRI scan)
- Infectious disease screening
- Psychological evaluation
- Social work assessment
- Financial counseling

### Step 3: Final Clearance (Week 2-3)
- Review by transplant committee
- Final medical clearance
- Consent documentation
- Surgery scheduling

## 3. CONSENT REQUIREMENTS

### Mandatory Documents
1. **Informed Consent Form** - Detailed explanation of risks and benefits
2. **Medical History Declaration** - Complete disclosure of health conditions
3. **SOP Acknowledgment** - Confirmation of understanding procedures
4. **Emergency Contact Authorization** - Designated contact person
5. **Post-Donation Care Agreement** - Commitment to follow-up care

### Consent Must Include
- Understanding of surgical risks
- Awareness of recovery period
- Knowledge of potential complications
- Agreement to post-operative care
- Right to withdraw consent before surgery

## 4. PRE-DONATION PROCEDURES

### Medical Preparation
- Fasting 8-12 hours before surgery
- Stop certain medications as advised
- Complete pre-operative tests
- Anesthesia consultation
- Blood cross-matching

### Administrative Steps
- Hospital admission 1 day before surgery
- Final consent verification
- Insurance documentation
- Donor registry enrollment
- Family counseling session

## 5. SURGICAL PROCEDURE

### Kidney Donation
- Laparoscopic nephrectomy (minimally invasive)
- Surgery duration: 2-3 hours
- Hospital stay: 2-3 days
- Recovery period: 4-6 weeks

### Liver Donation
- Open or laparoscopic hepatectomy
- Surgery duration: 4-6 hours
- Hospital stay: 5-7 days
- Recovery period: 8-12 weeks

### Heart Donation (Deceased Only)
- Performed after brain death declaration
- Multi-organ retrieval surgery
- Coordinated with transplant network
- Time-sensitive procedure (4-6 hours from retrieval to transplant)

## 6. POST-DONATION CARE

### Immediate Post-Operative (0-7 days)
- ICU monitoring (if required)
- Pain management
- Wound care
- Early mobilization
- Dietary management

### Short-Term Recovery (1-3 months)
- Weekly follow-up visits
- Blood tests monitoring
- Physical therapy if needed
- Gradual return to activities
- Psychological support

### Long-Term Follow-Up (Lifelong)
- Annual health check-ups
- Kidney function monitoring (for kidney donors)
- Liver function tests (for liver donors)
- Lifestyle counseling
- Donor registry updates

## 7. RISKS AND COMPLICATIONS

### General Surgical Risks
- Bleeding
- Infection
- Blood clots
- Anesthesia complications
- Wound healing issues

### Organ-Specific Risks

#### Kidney Donation
- Reduced kidney function (rare)
- High blood pressure (5-10% risk)
- Protein in urine
- Hernia at incision site

#### Liver Donation
- Bile leak (5-10% risk)
- Liver failure (rare, <1%)
- Infection
- Blood clots
- Prolonged recovery

#### Heart Donation
- N/A (deceased donation only)

## 8. DONOR RIGHTS

### Legal Rights
- Right to withdraw consent anytime before surgery
- Right to confidentiality
- Right to medical care and follow-up
- Protection from discrimination
- Legal protection under organ donation laws

### Financial Rights
- No payment for organ donation
- Hospital costs covered by recipient's insurance
- Lost wages compensation (varies by region)
- Travel and accommodation support
- Post-operative care coverage

## 9. ETHICAL GUIDELINES

### Prohibited Practices
- Commercial trading of organs
- Coercion or pressure to donate
- Donation by minors (except specific cases)
- Donation under influence of drugs/alcohol
- False medical information

### Ethical Standards
- Voluntary informed consent
- Equitable organ allocation
- Transparency in process
- Respect for donor autonomy
- Confidentiality maintenance

## 10. EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

### During Surgery
- Immediate conversion to open surgery if complications
- Blood transfusion availability
- ICU bed reserved
- Specialist surgeons on standby

### Post-Operative Emergencies
- 24/7 emergency contact number
- Readmission protocol
- Complication management team
- Psychological crisis support

## 11. QUALITY ASSURANCE

### Hospital Requirements
- Accredited transplant center
- Experienced surgical team
- ICU facilities
- 24/7 laboratory services
- Ethics committee oversight

### Documentation Standards
- Complete medical records
- Consent forms archived
- Surgical reports detailed
- Follow-up records maintained
- Donor registry updated
